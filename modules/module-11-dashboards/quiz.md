# Quiz - Review questions and answers

## Questions

1. Why should dashboards be designed around audience?
2. What is an operational question?
3. Why are consistent units important?
4. What makes a dashboard useful during an incident?
5. When should a panel be removed?
6. Why should dashboards link to deeper evidence?
7. What is the risk of copying dashboards between services?

## Answers

1. Different audiences need different detail, context and decisions.
2. An operational question is something the dashboard should help answer during normal operation or incident response.
3. Consistent units reduce confusion and comparison errors.
4. It highlights symptoms quickly and points to likely investigation paths.
5. Remove a panel when it no longer supports a decision or is never used.
6. Dashboards show signals; deeper evidence usually comes from traces, logs or detailed queries.
7. Copied dashboards may not match the service's real risks, dependencies or ownership model.
