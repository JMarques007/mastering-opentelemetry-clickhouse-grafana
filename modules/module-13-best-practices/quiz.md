# Quiz - Review questions and answers

## Questions

1. Why should telemetry design start with questions?
2. What is signal quality?
3. How can high cardinality affect cost?
4. Why is telemetry privacy important?
5. What happens to unowned dashboards and alerts?
6. What is the difference between cost control and losing evidence?
7. Why should observability assets be reviewed regularly?

## Answers

1. Questions ensure telemetry supports decisions rather than becoming noise.
2. Signal quality means telemetry is consistent, contextual, safe and actionable.
3. High cardinality creates many unique series or records, increasing storage and query cost.
4. Telemetry can contain sensitive data and must follow privacy and security expectations.
5. They decay, become noisy and lose trust.
6. Cost control keeps useful evidence intentionally; careless removal loses diagnostic value.
7. Services, ownership, queries and risks change over time.
