# Quiz - Review questions and answers

## Questions

1. What makes an alert actionable?
2. Why is ownership important?
3. How can short evaluation windows create noise?
4. What should an alert message include?
5. Why should alerts be reviewed regularly?
6. What is alert fatigue?
7. Why should alerts link to runbooks?

## Answers

1. It has a clear symptom, impact, owner and next step.
2. Owned alerts are routed, maintained and acted on by the right team.
3. Short windows may fire on brief spikes that do not require human response.
4. It should include symptom, service, environment, severity, owner and investigation links.
5. Systems and teams change, so old thresholds and routes can become inaccurate.
6. Alert fatigue is the loss of trust caused by too many noisy or low-value alerts.
7. Runbooks turn alert context into response action.
